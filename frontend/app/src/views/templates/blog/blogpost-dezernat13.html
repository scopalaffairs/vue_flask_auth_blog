In-App Purchases

Curatory,performance painting
by Dalien Schanhair, March 16, 2015

Schanhair’s performance paint act In-App Purchases could be seen as an analogism of the drift of digital and modular buying formats. The performance renders a broad line of ambiguity between spoof and psychedelic art. Performing artists usually do not share static content as their expression is time-based. This confines to a smaller amount of opportunities which is broken hereby—dance, act and paint come together in a ritual of self-disclosure and avartarism. The contextual sense seems like a multi-medial persiflage on business models of software startups bargaining and baiting recipients into contracts and the bloating of prices for artworks. The background piece is accessible and pre-viewable, yet constrained to white spaces. The modules can be unlocked for viewing. The original modules are not for sale as they are reproduced digitally and then burned. With this strategy, we could see painting and performance moved into an area of consumable entertainment.

Which could be disgusting, if not the model of Software as a Service prohibited ownership rights—buyers can not own the product or entity, but rather gain a copy for a range of time. This could act like a marketing persiflage on art selling itself and could open pensive areas embodying the changing laws of ownerships of art in Europe and their selling and the absurdity added to it—after all the sentence “all art made by mankind should belong to everybody” could be right, if France was just a pretty frame for the Mona Lisa, since she is Italian.

If art may sell itself and yet remain art, Shanhair’s works are a statement between street-art and oh-so-serious artists showing bold citations in a stencil manner, yet with a sensitive touch, like a tattoo-artist covering up your pretty little bunny on your butt—with a giant and hungry shark, because—you know—you always liked sharks, didn’t you?
