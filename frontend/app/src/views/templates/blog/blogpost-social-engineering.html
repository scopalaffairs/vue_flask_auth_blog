Social Engineering

experimental,sound-installation
by daniel herrmann, August 30, 2012

“Das Fehlen sichtbarer Gewalt erlaubt der Manipulation sich als jene Freiheit auszugeben, die sie entzieht" —Friedrich Hacker
“The lack of visible violence enables manipulation to masquerade as the very freedom it deprives”.

Three exhibition vicinities, each covering specific forms of manipulation, in one gallery space.
Social Engineering with Chan Sook Choi, Christine Gottschalk and Daniel Herrmann. Exhibiton, Auguststrasse 89, Berlin, June 2012

Sound installation, Berlin. We developed a scenographic transitional zone, working as a socio-reflective junction between the two other participants. The spatial sound is recorded and stratified with atmospheres. In a sensorial field, that is marked out, visitors interact en passant.
Their motion triggers recently recorded moments arbitrarily. Furthermore the synthesizer, that creates the atmosphere is modulated according to the position in the sensorial field.
Nested moments of recorded moments in recorded moments are triggered momentarily. Now could also be regarded as a cumulation of by-gone moments.

Example samples of the exhibition at Auguststrasse 89, June 2012
